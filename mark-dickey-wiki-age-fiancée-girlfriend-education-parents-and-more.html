All About American Caver Mark Dickey

Mark Dickey (age: 40, born in 1983) is an American caver and Speleologist trapped 3000 feet deep within the intricate Turkish cave known as Morca.

During an expedition in the Morca cave, situated in the picturesque Taurus Mountains of southern Turkey, Mark and a small group, including three other Americans, unexpectedly fell ill.

Mark’s health took a worrisome turn as he began experiencing gastrointestinal issues, leading to bleeding and fluid loss from his stomach. However, there’s a glimmer of hope amidst this dire situation. After several days, Mark ceased vomiting and managed to eat a meal, signaling a potential improvement in his condition.

He courageously recorded a video message from the cave’s depths to express his gratitude to all those involved in the complex rescue operation. Mark specifically thanked the dedicated doctors who provided crucial medical assistance, ultimately saving his life.

The magnitude of this rescue operation is truly unprecedented, with over 170 rescuers from six countries coming together to undertake this monumental task.

Despite the lingering uncertainty surrounding the exact cause of Mark’s severe stomach ailment, rescuers remain optimistic that they will soon successfully bring Mark back to the surface, reuniting him with his loved ones.

World’s Largest Cave Rescue Operation

A multinational rescue operation is in full swing to aid the leader of a cave rescue group based in North Jersey, who remains stranded deep within a Turkish cave after falling seriously ill last week.

Mark Dickey, who serves as the chief of the New Jersey Initial Response Team headquartered in Sussex County, has been trapped underground in Turkey’s Morca cave for approximately two-thirds of a mile (over 1,000 meters) since August 31, as reported by the organization’s Facebook post and confirmed by the Turkish government.

This 40-year-old cave expert was actively participating in the exploration of the cave when he encountered severe intestinal issues. Unfortunately, these issues swiftly escalated into a life-threatening condition characterized by severe bleeding and vomiting, as detailed in the organization’s social media update.

Mark’s ailment has rendered him unable to navigate the cold and damp cave terrain under his power, making it a time-sensitive and challenging situation. Rescue teams comprising experienced cavers must endure roughly eight hours of rugged terrain to reach Mark’s location.

Additionally, communication between the rescue teams at the surface and Mark, who remains deep within the cave, has been hampered by the inoperable phone lines within the cave system, further complicating the rescue effort.

Mark Dickey is Engaged to Jessica Van Ord

The Morca exploration journey for Mark Dickey began in August 2023. He was so excited about the exploration that he posted a photo on his Facebook profile in which he and his crew were seen checking all his tools.

Sadly, the journey, which was meant to be adventurous, became hellish for the NY native when he developed a medical condition causing blood-filled pukes.

Mark Dickey’s Early Life and Background

In 1983, Mark Dickey (age 40) was born in Croton-on-Hudson, New York, America. Mark was a teenager when he started caving. Over the years, he explored caves throughout the USA and the world.

He boasts of caving in over 20 different states and 10 different countries. Most of his trips ranged from casual horizontal beginner trips to month-long expeditions. In 2009, he started taking cave rescue courses and became a National Cave Rescue Commission Instructor four years later.

Mark’s Facebook profile shows he is a National Instructor at NCRC (National Cave Rescue Commission), Medical Commission Secretary at ECRA (European Cave Rescue Association), and Executive Director at Caving Academy.
