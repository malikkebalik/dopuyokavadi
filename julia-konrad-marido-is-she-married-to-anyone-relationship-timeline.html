Learn More About Julia Konrad’s Career Life Explored

Born in Recife, Pernambuco, Julia spent most of her childhood in Buenos Aires, Argentina, where her family lived for a decade. 

Julia made her television debut in Brazil as Janaína, a singer-songwriter in the Globo soap opera Now Generation. She later appeared in several guest roles in other soap operas on the same network before landing her breakthrough role as Ciça.

In 2019, Julia played the role of Ruth-Céline Japhet, a medium, in the film Kardec, directed by Wagner de Assis. She then joined the cast of O Sétimo Guardião, a television series on Rede Globo, where she played Raimunda.

In 2020, Julia reprised her role as Pepita in the fourth and final season of One Against All and released her first single, “Vuelve,” as part of the series soundtrack. She later collaborated with Pernambuco musician Barro to release her second single, “Colores.”

That same year, Julia bravely shared her experience as a survivor of sexual abuse, raising awareness about marital rape in Brazil and starting a movement that empowers survivors. 

Her story, published in Claudia magazine, led to other actresses speaking out about their own experiences of abuse. The publication established a permanent channel where women could share their stories and receive legal and psychological support.

In 2021, Julia starred as anthropologist Gabriela Alves in the first season of the Brazilian Netflix series Invisible City. She also landed the role of Paloma, an Interpol agent working undercover, in the Amazon Prime Video Brazilian original series Dom.
