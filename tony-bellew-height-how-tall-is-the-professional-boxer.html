Tony Bellew Height: How Tall is the Professional Boxer?

Publish date: 2024-10-01

Tony Bellew is an English professional boxer. He competed from 2007 to 2018 and now works as a boxing analyst and commentator. He won the WBC cruiserweight title in 2016 and held it until 2017.

In 2018, he tried for the undisputed cruiserweight championship. Before that, at a lower level, he was the British and Commonwealth light-heavyweight champion from 2010 to 2014 and the European cruiserweight champion from 2015 to 2016.

As an amateur, he won the ABA heavyweight championship three times. In movies, he played the role of the rival boxer Ricky Conlan in the films Creed and Creed III.

However, his height is a topic of interest among fans. In this post, you will get all the information related to his age and height, as well as more updates related to his professional life.

What is Tony Bellew Height?

Tony Bellew’s height is 6 feet 3 inches (191 cm). This physical attribute has been a significant factor in his career as a professional boxer, allowing him to leverage his reach and size advantage in the ring.

What is Tony Bellew Age?

Tony Bellew is 41 years old. He was born in Toxteth, Liverpool, on November 30, 1982. His mom is black, and his dad is white. He initially lived on Mulgrave Street in Toxteth and later moved to nearby Smithdown Road in Wavertree.

Tony Bellew’s Career Highlights and Achievements

  • Anthony Bellew started his professional boxing career on October 6, 2007, with a second-round TKO against Jamie Ambler. In 2008, he continued to win against opponents like Adam Wilcox and Wayne Brooks, maintaining an unbeaten record. By 2009, Bellew was 12-0, defeating fighters like Matthew Ellis and Nick Okoth.
  • In March 2010, Bellew won the vacant Commonwealth light-heavyweight title by beating Atoli Moore. He successfully defended the title against Bob Ajisafe in September 2010 and Ovill McKenzie in December 2010, overcoming knockdowns in the latter fight.
  • In October 2011, Bellew challenged Nathan Cleverly for the WBO light-heavyweight title but lost via majority decision. He rebounded by winning the British and Commonwealth titles in a rematch against Ovill McKenzie.
  • Bellew moved up to cruiserweight and won the WBO International title in March 2014. He continued his success with a title defense against Julio Cesar Dos Santos in July 2014.
  • In November 2014, Bellew secured a rematch against Nathan Cleverly, this time as cruiserweight. Bellew won via split decision, and the victory helped settle their rivalry.
  • Bellew challenged for the WBC cruiserweight title in May 2016, facing Ilunga Makabu. Despite a first-round knockdown, Bellew knocked out Makabu to become the WBC champion.
  • Moving to heavyweight, Bellew faced David Haye in March 2017 and won via TKO after Haye suffered an ankle injury. They had a rematch in May 2018, with Bellew winning by TKO in the fifth round again.
  • In November 2018, Bellew faced undisputed cruiserweight champion Oleksandr Usyk. Usyk won the fight in the eighth round, and after the match, Bellew announced his retirement from boxing.
