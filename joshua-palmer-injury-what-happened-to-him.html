When Did Joshua Palmer Start Playing Football?

Palmer, the son of Keith and Alethia Palmer was raised in Brampton, Ontario. Keiana and Lanisha are his sisters. In addition to playing football, he ran track and played basketball at St. Roch Catholic Secondary School. In the tenth grade, he received the “St. Roch’s Junior Athlete of the Year” award.

In the ninth and tenth grades, he played wide receiver and defensive back to assist St. Roch in winning two JV football crowns. In addition, he was a player with the Brampton Bulldogs and the Ontario Football Conference’s Burlington Stampeders.

Palmer attended summer football clinics across the country and was recruited by multiple Division I football organizations. He then spent his last two years of high school at St. Thomas Aquinas High School in Fort Lauderdale, Florida.

Palmer was a three-star prospect when he made his collegiate football commitment at Syracuse in the summer prior to his senior year. With 32 receptions for 506 yards and eight touchdowns at the end of his senior year, he was chosen to the second team of the Class 7A All-State.

He also played during his college life and after that he started playing professionally. He did very hard work to make his name.
